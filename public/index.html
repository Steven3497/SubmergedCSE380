OVERVIEW

Submerged is a single player 2D game where the player (a deep sea diver) is frantically swimming to escape the jaws of a monster chasing them. Being constrained by the walls of an unusual underwater cave, the player must evade obstacles and spikes in order in hopes of surviving the pursuit. Once the diver has reached the end of the cave, they are faced with a new challenge in which the monster evolves into an amphibious abomination and the player has to find a way to defeat the creature. Without any weapons, the player has to look for an alternative, resourceful solution; perhaps in their environment…

TECHNOLOGY

Submerged will be developed for the Windows Platform using the McKilla's Gorilla game engine, which is a bare-bones engine developed by Professor Richard Mckenna for rapid prototyping 2D games. DirectX will be used as the underlying rendering technology. Chiptone and Audacity will be used for all music and sound effects management. Artwork will be done using Piskel.

STORY

The deep sea is not a place for the weak-hearted. You are surrounded by darkness, laying mercy to the mysterious creatures watching you from the depths of the abyss. With 80% of the oceans left unexplored, who knows what kind of abominations reside in these realms. You are a curious diver, seeking answers and chasing adrenaline, only this time, you may have bitten off more than you can chew. With the help of advanced medicines granting you the ability to withstand the immense pressure of the deep ocean, you venture far from the safety of the shore and the comfort of the surface. As you sink downwards, you pass by the occasional sea turtles and parrotfish but eventually, the light fades from your eyes until it becomes nothing but a dim void encapsulating your body. As you explore, you feel the water push and sway around your body. Quickly, you jut around looking for the source, only to be greeted by the darkness of the deep ocean once again. Your heart begins to race as anxieties overtake your mind, but as an experienced diver, you bring yourself back to your senses and continue on. Soon enough, you encounter a dark cave with spiked structures along the edges. As you are about to enter, you feel a strong shock of water collide with your body and spin around. Fumbling for your emergency flashlight, you switch on the remote and your eyes widen in fear. An enormous monster with razor sharp teeth gapes open its mouth ready to make you its next meal. With no time wasted, you dodge the monster’s attack and dart for the cave. Within seconds, you find yourself swimming for your life.

The objective of Submerged is to navigate the player through the underwater cave and defeat the monster without dying.

GAMEPLAY

When the player begins the game, they are presented with an ominous underwater cave where they are in control of the terrified diver who has to swim rightwards, away from the pursuing monster. The screen will follow the player as they venture and present new obstacles and hazards within the cave that the player must avoid. If the player survives until the end of the cave, the scrolling will stop and the monster will morph into a terrifying amphibious abomination. From there, the player can no longer run away, and has to use their environment in order to defeat the monster. The player wins if they are able to successfully kill the monster.

CONTROLS

Right Arrow Key: Swim/Walk Right Left Arrow Key: Swim/Walk Left Up Arrow Key: Swim Up Down Arrow Key: Swim Down Space Bar: Jump ESC: Pause/Resume. If a game is not in progress, ESC does nothing

GRAPHICAL USER INTERFACE

Splash Screen: The splash screen GUI presents a game logo, background art, a level selection button, controls button, and help button. In-Game Menu: While the game is in progress, we'll always have a game menu that displays the gameplay controls again and allows the player to select from the following options: ESC: Resume Q: Quit to main menu In-Game GUI: Once the game begins, the only display on the screen besides the game itself is a timer which keeps track of the amount of time has elapsed since the game has began.

ARTWORK

All artwork in the game will be original. The following needs to be created. Diver: The diver will be dressed in typical underwater scuba diving gear and have the following animations: Swimming Right Swimming Left Swimming Up Swimming Down Walking Right Walking Left Jumping Taking Damage Dying Dead Monster: The monster will have two phases: shark phase and amphibious phase. The monster will have the following animations: Chomping Right Walking Right Walking Left Jumping Attacking Right Attacking Left Taking Damage Dying Dead Spikes/Triggers: The player will take damage if they touch the spikes. The player will trigger an event that could cause damage to the monster in the boss battle if they touch a trigger. For these non-collidable tiles, we will use specific tiles from the tileset in order to indicate to the player that these are interactable and for the purpose of implementing the corresponding events. Background Tiles: For non-collidable tiles in the background, we will simply arrange tiles from the tileset in various patterns.

SOUND EFFECTS

All sound effects will be original. The following sound effects will be required: Swimming Landing Player Takes Damage Player Dies Monster Takes Damage Monster Dies Falling Spike Collides Trigger Hit

MUSIC

All music will be created using sounds generated from chiptone, audacity, and ourselves. There will be two main tracks as of now: one for the menu screen/main game and one for the boss battle.
